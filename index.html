About

Web developer, connoisseur of the pun and lover of puzzles. As a Web Dev I have the privilege of being able to dedicate my time to two of my most-loved activities, developing new neural pathways, and problem solving. I have a theatre background that taught me the intricacies of collaborating, risk taking, and using criticism as a new challenge rather than a defeat. After spending time drumming and spitting paint on canvases in the Blue Man Group, I sought a life change and an opportunity to problem solve on a community level. What followed next was a year spent managing the volunteer pool of the largest volunteer mobilizer in New England, working extensively in Salesforce and CMS programs and growing its engagement to levels not seen in the last decade. The deeper I got into work with CMS tools and web work, the more frustrated I became that I had to rely on tools that others had built for me. Never being one to sit on the sidelines and let others do the hard work for me, I dove head first into web development so that I could build the tools that the web needs rather than wondering why they haven't been built yet. With a strong background of collaboration and creative problem solving, web development allows me to take my skills and apply them on a more powerful scale than I'd imagined possible. I love collaborating with like-minded individuals who meet challenges head-on, solve problems in creative and beautiful ways and who never stop being excited to learn new things.

Nozama.com

Nozama.com is an e-commerce site developed as part of group project during my time at General Assembly. This project provided valuable experience in pair-programming, SCRUM, and collaborative development. My primary responsibility during creation of Nozama was building a robust API in Node.js the could accommodate a variety of user needs.

  • Date: October 2018
  • Category: Back-end Development (Node.js)

The Hammer

Click the screenshot to see the live site

The Hammer is a tool inventory management system inspired by the need for easy-to-use, accessible, and cost effective IMSs in the non-profit sector. This full stack application features a Rails API hosted through Heroku and a Javascript frontend. The Hammer rapidly evolved into a css animation playground for me and it's where I honed my skills working with flexbox, keyframes and transitions

  • Date: September 2018
  • Category: Fullstack(Front-End Focus)

Tic Tac Toe

Click the screenshot to see the live site

The project that began it all. Tic Tac Toe was my first adventure into web developemnt. Built with a JavaScript front end and connecting to a third party API, Tic Tac Toe, provided my first insight into the complexity of taking a simple task for most humans and breaking that task down into something a computer can understand and work with.

  • Date: August 2018
  • Category: Front-End

Weather or Not

Click the screenshot to see the live site

Weather or Not is a weather app focused on answering a very simple question: 'do I need an umbrella today or not?'. Built with a React.js frontend, a Ruby back-end and integrating the Dark Sky API, Weather or Not analyzes weather information and then abstracts that data away from the user to provide them with the simplest 'yes' or 'no' response.

  • Date: October 2018
  • Category: Full-Stack (React.js/Ruby on Rails)
